Become a Steward of Nature

Our mission is to bring individuals together in preserving and enhancing the environment through local volunteer efforts focused on sustainability and cleanliness focused on tree planting, coastal cleaning, and sidewalk cleaning in every community we serve, fostering a cleaner and more sustainable world for all.

Here's how you can take part:

  1. Take a look at the volunteer options
  2. Select the one you are interested in
  3. Check the locations near you
  4. Select a date and time to participate
  5. Become a steward of nature!

Tree Planting

Tree Planting

Trees are essential to a healthy environment. Through this activity, volunteers can help create green spaces in communities by planting a variety of native trees and plants. This contributes to air purification, improves biodiversity, and provides long-term benefits for future generations. Whether you're experienced or completely new, you’ll be guided by team leaders and supplied with the tools you need. Expect a fulfilling day of teamwork and growth—both in trees and in impact. Join us and become part of something that lasts for decades, one tree at a time.

Coastal Cleaning

Coastal Cleaning

Plastic waste and debris along the coast not only pollute our oceans but endanger countless marine species. This activity invites volunteers to spend a few hours clearing beaches of litter, sorting recyclables, and raising awareness about ocean conservation. It’s a hands-on way to learn about marine ecosystems while making a real difference in preserving their beauty. Volunteers will be grouped in teams, with guidance provided on how to safely collect and dispose of trash. Protect our shores, preserve biodiversity, and help ensure cleaner waters for generations to come.

Sidewalk Clean-up

Sidewalk Clean-up

Our sidewalks are often overlooked when it comes to environmental responsibility. This initiative aims to keep public walkways free from litter and debris. Volunteers will assist in sweeping, trash collecting, and even repainting curbs in some areas. A clean sidewalk promotes community pride, improves accessibility, and supports better public health. This event is perfect for those who want to give back locally with immediate, visible results. With every bag filled, your group will help create safer and more pleasant routes for pedestrians and residents alike. Join us in making our streets shine.

FAQs

Who can volunteer for cleanup events? Anyone! Whether you’re a student (with approval of guardians), working professional, or resident of Bacolod, as long as you’re willing to help, you are welcome to join.

Is there a dress code? We recommend wearing comfortable clothes you don’t mind getting dirty, closed shoes, a hat or cap, and wear sunscreen to protect yourself from the sun. Don't also to bring your own water to stay hydrated!

Will I be provided with cleaning tools? Yes, we provide basic cleaning tools such as gloves, broom, and trash bags. However, feel free to bring your own if you prefer.

Will food or water be provided? We recommend bringing your own reusable water bottles. Light snacks may be provided depending on the sponsor for that date, but it’s best to come prepared.

Will I receive a certificate? Yes! Volunteers who participate will receive a digital certificate of participation, which is great for school, work, or community service records.
